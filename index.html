Easy Mexican Meal

Serves 4 - 45 mins

This dish always amazes my husband that so little goes into making a really tasty dish. This is one of theses dishes I will put on between getting home from work and taking children to sporting activities after school(I just turn the heat in the oven down if I know I will be away for over an hour).

If you are not sure what Chipotle Chilles in Adobo Sauce or Mexican Oregano is please read quick explanation on my Mexican recipe page.


Ingredients

  • 2 tbs Olive Oil
  • 4 Chicken breasts
  • 2 large brown Onions slice into half rings
  • 2 dried Chipotle Chillies soaked for an hour in water then chopped into large chunks or 2 chipotle chillies in adobo sauce from can
  • 100 ml of chicken stock
  • tbs of dried Mexican Oregano if available or dried Thyme
  • Salt and Pepper

Method

Step One

Blend the Chipotle chilles and chicken stock together - this can be done well in advance if you are organised.

Step Two

Heat your oven to 180 degrees Centigrade

Step Three

In dutch oven(because I have one) or oven proof dish gently fry onions until slightly brown on edges, around 10 minutes.

Step Four

Place chicken on top of onions and pour over sauce.

Step Five

Sprinkle with the dried herbs and place in oven for about 35-45 minutes ensuring that the chicken is properly cooked.

Tips

I serve this with Mexican Black Beans(see website), cabbage and coriander salad(see below) and avacado.


Any leftovers are great in a toasted sandwich the next day with cheese and corn and red onion.

Family recipe

Versatile Tomato Sauce

This sauce is made once a fortnight in our house, I make a big batch and use when needed. My kids will eat it just with Pasta and Parmesan(or cheddar if I have none, they don't care) which is a great mid week meal for when you are in a rush but still want to feed your family a decent meal.

NOTE - - makes a around 3 meals worth, to be split up and used when required


Ingredients

  • 2 medium brown onion roughly chopped
  • sprinkle of chilli flakes
  • 2 clove of garlic finely chopped or grated
  • 800 grams of tinned diced tomatoes)
  • 2tbs of fresh Thyme or 1tbs of dried thyme
  • salt and pepper
  • sugar if needed

Method

Step One

Heat up heavy bottom pot add olive oil and fry onion, garlic and chilli until soft and translucent.

Step Two

Add tinned tomatoes and thyme and bring to simmer stirring to ensure tomatoes do not stick to bottom of pan

Step Three

Leave to barely simmer on low heat stirring occasionally for 30 mins to 2 hours dependant on time and intensity of flavour

Step Four

Check for seasoning, if the tomatoes are bitter they may need a small amount of sugar to sweeten the sauce.

Step Five

Optional - Blend if you do not like chunky bits of tomato(like my daughter)


Tips

This sauce can be kept in the fridge for up to 7 days and frozen once cooled to be used for many other dishes - I use this with meatballs.(my recipe on the European page)

The recipe can be used as Pizza sauce and is also really good with some fried Chirozo sausage,bacon or salami and peas with your choice of pasta.
